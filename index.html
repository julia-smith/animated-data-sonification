This animated data sonification illustrates the loss of life due to mass shootings in the United States since 1982. It relies on the definition of "mass murder" found in the FBI's crime classification report: an incident with four or more fatalities (excluding the shooter) in a single event, typically in a single location.

Each fatality is represented by a single orb in the illustration and a single note in the audio track. The time between clusters of notes is proportional to the time between each mass shooting incident.
